Post-Surgery Care Guide

Published on Aug 27, 2025 | By SAH Ortho

Recovering from orthopedic surgery requires patience, discipline, and the right approach. Proper post-surgery care ensures faster recovery, reduces the risk of complications, and improves long-term results.

This comprehensive guide outlines key strategies for effective healing—from pain management and nutrition to mobility exercises and emotional well-being—helping you regain strength and confidence after surgery.

Recovery & Care

Follow Doctor’s Instructions

Always adhere to the prescribed medication schedule, wound care, and physical therapy routines. Skipping appointments or stopping medicines early can lead to infections or delayed healing.

Maintain clear communication with your surgeon regarding symptoms like swelling, fever, or persistent pain.

Manage Pain and Swelling

  • Use pain relief medications as prescribed by your doctor.
  • Apply ice packs for 15–20 minutes several times a day.
  • Elevate the affected limb to reduce swelling and improve blood flow.

Avoid applying heat during the initial recovery days unless advised.

Nutrition and Hydration

Eat a balanced diet rich in protein, calcium, vitamin D, and iron to strengthen tissues and bones. Include leafy greens, dairy, lentils, and lean proteins in your daily meals.

Stay hydrated—water aids nutrient absorption and detoxification.

Physical Therapy

Engage in rehabilitation exercises prescribed by your physiotherapist to restore strength and mobility. Consistency and correct posture are key to successful recovery.

Avoid overexertion — gradual progress is safer and more sustainable.

Prevent Infections

Keep your surgical wound clean and dry. Follow dressing instructions carefully and monitor for signs of infection like redness or unusual discharge.

Wash your hands regularly and avoid touching the incision area unnecessarily.

Emotional Well-being

Recovery can be mentally exhausting. Surround yourself with positivity, talk to loved ones, and engage in light activities that bring joy and motivation.

Meditation or short walks can significantly improve your mood and reduce stress.

With consistent care, patience, and proper guidance, your post-surgery recovery can be smooth and successful — helping you return to your daily life stronger than before.
