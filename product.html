TECHNOLOGY

BASIC PRINCIPLE

The TIC (Titanium Inlay Clip) System allows for variable angle (VA) screw locking within the Bonebridge osteosynthesis plates. The TIC System combines two metals (pure titanium and medical grade stainless steel), both known for their differences in stiffness and strength properties. The softer titanium is used to manufacture the TIC inlay, which is firmly assembled to the plate body. The harder stainless steel screw then forms its thread into the TIC inlay during the insertion of the VA Locking Screw. The screw then locks at a predefined locking torque, resulting in a fixed screw to plate construct.

The TIC has shown consistently low removal torques after mechanical lifecycle tests as well as in clinical use. Despite this, results show equal to superior stability compared to competitors. The combination of both metals in orthopaedic implants is proven to be biocompatible.

15° ALL AROUND

As the VA Locking Screw forms its own thread into the TIC inlay, the screw may be angulated in any direction within the cone of operation (+/-15°). If needed, a VA Locking Screw can be inserted up to 3 times into the same hole.

ONE HOLE, TWO SCREWS

All TIC holes can be used with either VA Locking Screws or Cortical Screws, depending on the specific surgical needs.

ONE CLICK TO STABILITY

Thanks to its unique geometry, the TIC allows all drill guides to click into the desired hole for stable drilling support.
